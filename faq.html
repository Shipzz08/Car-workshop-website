Frequently Asked Questions

We recommend changing your car's oil every 3,000 to 5,000 miles or as specified in your vehicle's owner's manual. Regular oil changes are crucial to maintaining your engine's performance and longevity.

Routine maintenance, including oil changes, brake inspections, tire rotations, and fluid checks, is essential. Following the manufacturer's recommended service schedule is the best way to ensure your car runs smoothly and lasts longer.

If you notice any of the following signs, it's time to have your brakes checked: squeaking or grinding noises, reduced braking performance, a soft brake pedal, or a pulsating sensation when you apply the brakes. Regular brake inspections can catch issues before they become major problems..

If your check engine light illuminates, it's best to schedule a diagnostic check as soon as possible. This light can indicate various issues, from minor to serious. Ignoring it can lead to more extensive and costly repairs down the road. We have the equipment and expertise to diagnose and resolve the issue.

While we primarily use high-quality, manufacturer-recommended parts, we can sometimes accommodate customers who prefer to provide their own parts. However, we advise discussing this with our team beforehand to ensure compatibility and warranty coverage.
